## Before You Begin

- **Over-ear headphones or both earbuds.** Neurima only works when left and right stay separated. Speakers, mono output, or a single earbud will not produce the effect.
- **Audio already on the device.** Neurima plays local audio and cannot stream.
- If you plan to use `Music`, use tracks that are either purchased from the iTunes Store and downloaded to the device or synced from the Apple Music app on Mac. Synced files are usually preferred because you can choose higher-quality copies such as `ALAC` or other lossless audio. Apple Music subscription downloads currently do not work because they remain DRM-protected subscription copies, even when they appear downloaded in the Music app. Purchased iTunes Store tracks do work.

## What to Listen To

![Playlist add-audio sources](_screenshots/playlist-add-sources.png)

- `Your audio`: Music, podcasts, audiobooks, or anything else you already know well.
- `Music`: Browse songs already in the device Music library, including iTunes Store purchases downloaded to the device and audio synced from the Apple Music app on Mac (requires optional Media Library access).
- `Files`: Import local audio from the system file picker.
- `Generate`: If nothing is ready yet, create a sample ambient track from the playlist add menu.
- [[Website/Guides/Sound Lab|Sound Lab]]: Create original ambient piano audio in Neurima and send it to the playlist.

## First-Session Defaults

- `Processing`: on
- `Behavior Mode`: `Continuous`
- `Channel Affinity`: `Automatic`
- `Calibration Profile`: default
- `Circadian`: off

The default pathways are `Relax`, `Balance`, and `Focus`. You can refine or replace them later in [[Website/Guides/Optimization, Pathways & Circadian|Optimization, Pathways & Circadian]].
